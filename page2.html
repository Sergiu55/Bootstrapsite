Exploring the Fascinating World of Elephant Trunks:

Size, Function, and Evolution

How Big Do They Get?

As the largest land mammal on the planet, it's no secret that African elephants are massive animals. Reaching between 10 and 13 feet tall and weighing between 2 and 7 tons, these creatures are enormous, so it's no surprise that their trunks are also quite large. On average, the trunk of an African elephant can reach up to 7 feet. Asian elephants also have trunks, though not as large. Elephants can lift 4% of their weight using their trunk. For a male elephant that is approximately 265 pounds.

Difference Between African and Asian Trunks

African elephants and Asian elephants are two separate species. As such, they have a number of key differences between their trunks. Besides being larger, African elephants have two fingers (or tips) at the end of their trunks, while Asian elephants have only one. The environmental conditions in the savanna render the African elephant's trunk harder than the Asian elephant's softer trunk for forested terrain.
