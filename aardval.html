Classification

Aardvarks are small pig-like mammals that are found inhabiting a wide range of different habitats throughout Africa, south of the Sahara. They are mostly nocturnal and spend their lives digging underground burrows to protect themselves from the heat of the African sun, and predators too. Their name originates from the Afrikaans language in South Africa and means "earth pig," due to their long snout and pig-like body. Aardvarks are unique among animals as they are the only surviving species in their family.

Origins and Evolution

The Aardvark (Orycteropus afer), also known as an earth pig, is a very unique animal in terms of its evolutionary makeup. It has been assessed as having the highest level of evolutionary distinctiveness, according to the EDGE scientific criteria. This is because it does not share a close evolutionary history with any other living species.

Fossil evidence suggests that aardvarks have existed for over 55 million years, proving their point of ancient evolution. Despite their appearance, they are not closely related to pigs or anteaters but are instead classified in their own order of mammals, known as Tubulidentata.
